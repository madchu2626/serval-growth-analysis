1. Key Insights

Enterprise security is the winning ICP

Top campaigns target CISOs and security leaders, or capture security-framed intent. Campaigns not targeting this audience or intent perform worse. The biggest opportunity is scaling campaigns that reach security decision-makers.

All campaigns drive strong pipeline ROAS

Even bottom performers drive 8–19x pipe ROAS. But efficiency matters: every dollar in Tier 3 could generate ~7x more pipeline if reallocated to Tier 1 campaigns.

Meta is underperforming

Meta drives 3–4x lower pipeline efficiency than Google or LinkedIn. Broad, non-ICP audience targeting is the culprit. Majority of Meta spend should be reallocated or repurposed for retargeting.

Google SEM is highly efficient

Google SEM is the strongest channel at 87x pipe ROAS and potential for untapped demand. Broad campaigns underperform vs. high-intent, suggesting opportunity to optimize toward bottom-funnel keywords.

5. Growth Experiment Design

Experiment 1

Intercept In-Market Buyers

Test paid campaigns built around intent signals to reach in-market buyers at the right moment.

Hypothesis

Current paid social campaigns underperform because they target broad, cold audiences that don’t match your enterprise ICP. Intent signals — both category-level (accounts researching ITSM, IT automation, security compliance) and competitor-level (accounts researching Moveworks, ServiceNow, Freshservice) — can identify buyers when they’re actively in-market. Campaigns built around these signals should produce higher-quality pipeline at a lower cost than always-on cold prospecting.

Budget Allocation

Pause bottom 3 Meta + bottom 2 LinkedIn campaigns ($46.5K spend). Reallocate 60% to top performers (~$28K). Use remaining ~$18.5K for signal-driven campaigns.

Campaign Structure

Category Intent

Channels

LinkedIn — ~$7.5K/mo

Audience

  • Target accounts surging on category intent topics via Clay/ZoomInfo/G2
  • Filter on firmographic criteria + job title alignment
  • Add these account lists as observation audiences in Google Ads to bid up on relevant searches
Competitor Intent

Channels

Google + LinkedIn — ~$8–10K/mo

Audience

  • Google SEM: Launch competitor campaigns targeting bottom-funnel competitor keywords. Drive to existing comparison pages.
  • Clay/ZoomInfo/G2 intent: Detect accounts researching competitors by name, push to LinkedIn with title targeting
  • Add competitor intent lists as observation audiences in Google Ads

Messaging & Creative

  • Category intent: Pain point and proof point creative informed by top-performing LinkedIn campaigns. Test sponsored content vs. conversation ads
  • Competitor Google SEM: Direct comparison ad copy → dedicated comparison landing pages
  • Competitor LinkedIn: Pain point and direct comparison messaging
Timeline 6–8 weeks, early read at 4 weeks
Measurement ROAS, pipe $/demo, cost per demo (compared against paused campaign benchmarks). Break out by signal type (category vs. competitor) and by competitor targeted. Success threshold: outperform paused Tier 3 campaigns (8–19x ROAS).
Experiment 2

Video Retargeting Engine

Test video retargeting across YouTube, LinkedIn, and Meta to see if this format shortens the conversion cycle and moves site visitors to demo faster than static ad retargeting.

Hypothesis

Video retargeting accelerates deal cycles. HockeyStack’s report found YouTube remarketing reduced average touchpoints to MQL by 7.1, and Facebook retargeting reduced touchpoints from MQL-to-SQL by 4.7. Serval already has video assets (customer testimonials, product demos). Testing video retargeting across YouTube, LinkedIn, and Meta could shorten the conversion cycle and move site visitors to demo faster than static ad retargeting.

Budget Allocation

$10–15K/month. YouTube (~$5K), LinkedIn (~$5K), Meta (~$3–5K).

Campaign Structure

  • Retarget site visitors and ad engagers with video content across all three platforms
  • Could segment by engagement depth: viewers who watch 50%+ get served a direct demo CTA as a follow-up ad
  • Test existing video assets first before investing in new production
  • Build sequential video funnels: awareness video → deeper product video → demo CTA

Messaging & Creative

  • Product page visitors: Customer testimonial videos
  • Pricing page visitors: Short product demo clips
  • Security ICP accounts: Security leader thought leadership content
Timeline 6–8 weeks, early read at 4 weeks
Measurement Incremental demos driven, cost per incremental demo, video completion rates, demo rate by engagement level (25% vs 50% vs 75%+ watched). Track touchpoints-to-conversion if attribution tooling allows. Compare video retargeting cost per demo against static retargeting benchmarks.

6. Growth Experiment Shortlist

Near-term

Persona-Specific Campaigns

Develop dedicated ad messaging and landing pages for ICPs like CISO, VP of Security, Directors of Security Operations, Head of IT, etc. speaking to individual pain points.

Near-term

New Channel Experimentation

Launch tests on Bing SEM, Reddit, X, ChatGPT/Perplexity ads, Clay/ZoomInfo ads, and YouTube retargeting to understand viability and potential for scale.

Near-term

Review Site Campaigns

Build out G2 profile and experiment with G2 ads. Test Capterra and other review platforms where enterprise buyers actively compare solutions.

Mid-term

ABM — Full Play

The intent signal campaign (Experiment 1) shows promise — scale into full ABM: build account lists on firmographic data, current tool usage, and intent signals, then target buyers with multi-channel campaigns personalized per account.

Longer-term

Industry-Specific Campaigns

Identify target industries and develop verticalized campaigns for tech, financial services, healthcare, defense, and government.
