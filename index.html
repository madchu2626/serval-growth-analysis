1. Key Insights

Enterprise security is the winning ICP

Top campaigns target CISOs and security leaders, or capture security-framed intent. Campaigns not targeting this audience or intent perform worse. The biggest opportunity is scaling campaigns that reach security decision-makers.

All campaigns drive strong pipeline ROAS

Even bottom performers drive 8–19x pipe ROAS. But efficiency matters: every dollar in Tier 3 could generate ~7x more pipeline if reallocated to Tier 1 campaigns.

Meta is underperforming

Meta drives 3–4x lower pipeline efficiency than Google or LinkedIn. Broad, non-ICP audience targeting is the culprit. Majority of Meta spend should be reallocated or repurposed for retargeting.

Google SEM is highly efficient

Google SEM is the strongest channel at 87x pipe ROAS and potential for untapped demand. Broad campaigns underperform vs. high-intent, suggesting opportunity to optimize toward bottom-funnel keywords.

5. Growth Experiment Design

Experiment 1

Turn Paid Social Into a Signal-Driven Channel

Pause low performing paid social campaigns.

Hypothesis

Cold prospecting on LinkedIn & Meta is driving underperformance relative to top campaigns. Current audience targeting is not reaching the enterprise ICP; paid social campaigns are reaching a non-ICP audience at lower seniority levels. Paid social dollars should be reallocated to convert warm audiences via retargeting and intent signal targeting.

Budget Allocation

Pause bottom 3 Meta + bottom 2 LinkedIn campaigns. Reallocate 60% of $46.5K to top performers (~$28K). Use remaining ~$18.5K for signal-driven campaigns: 60% retargeting (~$11K), 40% intent signals (~$7.5K).

Retargeting Campaign

Channels

LinkedIn & Meta — ~$11K/mo

Audience

  • Website visitors: Retarget by pageview segment (demo, product, pricing pages) with tailored ads
  • Ad engagers: Retarget engagers of other LinkedIn/Meta ads with new angles

Messaging & Creative

  • Demo page abandoners: Book a Demo CTA
  • Product page visitors: Social proof / case studies
  • Pricing page visitors: ROI messaging + Book a Demo
  • Ad engagers: New angle on original topic (ROI stat or proof point)
Intent Signal Campaign

Channels

LinkedIn — ~$7.5K/mo

Audience

  • Target leads at accounts crossing intent thresholds in Clay/ZoomInfo
  • Filter on firmographic criteria + job title alignment

Messaging & Creative

  • Pain point and proof point creative informed by top LinkedIn messaging
  • Test: (1) Sponsored content with soft CTA, (2) Conversation ads with branching CTAs
Timeline 6–8 weeks, early read at 4 weeks
Measurement Cost per demo, conversion lift vs. baseline, ROAS, pipe $/demo. Holdout test needed at scale for incrementality.
Experiment 2

Google SEM Competitor Campaign

Capture high-intent enterprise buyers actively searching competitor brand names.

Hypothesis

Significant high-intent demand exists in branded competitor searches. People searching competitors are more likely to be enterprise IT or security decision-makers, suggesting high-value leads that offset increased CPC.

Budget Allocation

$5–10K test budget to drive 8–15 demos/month. Higher cost per demo expected vs. existing campaigns, but lead quality and pipeline value should offset.

Campaign Structure

  • Target branded keywords for top competitors: ServiceNow, Moveworks, Freshservice, and others
  • Select competitors based on who Serval loses to most + search volume
  • One campaign per competitor for clean performance tracking

Messaging & Creative

  • Direct comparison and value prop messaging from ad copy to landing page
  • Leverage existing comparison pages on serval.com (vs. Jira SM, vs. Moveworks, vs. Freshservice)
  • Build additional pages for each targeted competitor
Timeline 4–6 weeks, directional read at 2–3 weeks
Success Threshold Outperform paused Tier 3 campaign benchmarks (8–19x ROAS)
Measurement ROAS, pipe $/demo, cost per demo — broken out by competitor targeted

6. Growth Experiment Shortlist

Additional experiments to test after validating the two primary experiments above. Ordered by proximity to current motion.

Near-term

Persona-Specific Campaigns

Develop dedicated ad messaging and landing pages for CISOs, VPs of Security, and Directors of Security Operations. The data shows security leaders convert best — generic messaging dilutes that signal. Persona-specific pages consistently outperform generic pages on demo conversion.

Near-term

New Channel Experimentation

Test emerging B2B channels with small budgets: Bing SEM (low competition, cheap clicks), Reddit (strong tech audience), direct mail for high-value enterprise accounts, and ChatGPT/Perplexity ads (early-mover advantage on AI search platforms). Also explore GEO/AEO optimization to ensure Serval surfaces in AI-generated search results where enterprise buyers increasingly start research.

Mid-term

ABM — Full Play

The intent signal campaign (Experiment 1) is ABM lite — behavioral signals to find in-market accounts. If it proves successful, scale into full ABM: named account lists built on firmographic data, current tool usage, and intent signals, with multi-channel campaigns (ads, email, direct mail) personalized per account tier. This is the natural progression, not a separate motion.

Longer-term

Industry-Specific Campaigns

Identify top-converting industries from segment data (once available) and build vertical-specific campaigns. Financial services, healthcare, and government are heavy security spenders. This expands the TAM beyond tech and creates defensible positioning per vertical.
